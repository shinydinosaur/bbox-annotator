Picasso Painting Annotation - Instructions

Welcome to our study! Thank you for participating. Below we provide useful instructions for annotating paintings. Please read them carefully and follow them as closely as you can during the study.

High level
For each painting you will be asked to do two things:
  1. Draw a bounding box around each person that appears in the painting.
  2. Tell us how hard or easy it was to identify the figures in the paintings as human (aka are they photorealistic?).
What to label?
All people in the paintings, unless:
  • You are unsure it is supposed to be a person (though some of them are wacky).
  • The person is very small (at your discretion).
  • Less than 10-20% of the person is visible.
Bounding box
Mark the bounding box of the visible area of the person (not the estimated total extent of the person). The bounding box should contain all visible pixels, except where the bounding box would have to be made excessively large to include a few additional pixels (less than 5% of the person).
